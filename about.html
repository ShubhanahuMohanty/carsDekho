  • Registration Certificate (RC):The RC is the most important document required to sell a used car. It is a legal document that proves ownership of the car.
  • Insurance Policy:The insurance policy is another important document required to sell a used car. It proves that the car is insured and that the buyer will be covered in case of an accident.
  • Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate:The PUC certificate is a document that proves that the car has passed the emission test. This is a mandatory requirement in most states in India.
  • Invoice:The invoice is a document that proves the purchase of the car. It is not mandatory to provide the invoice, but it can be helpful in proving the value of the car.
  • Service History:The service history is a document that shows that the car has been properly maintained. This can be helpful in proving the condition of the car.
  • Any other relevant documents: Depending on the state in which you are selling the car, you may need to provide other documents, such as a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the financier or a letter from the previous owner

  • Documentation: Gather all necessary paperwork related to your car, such as the Registration Certificate (RC), insurance policy, Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate, invoice, service history, and maintenance receipts. Keep them well-organised and easily accessible. Having a comprehensive documentation package will give potential buyers confidence in the car's history and condition
  • Clear the Clutter: Take the time to remove any personal items, such as documents and electronics. Washing the exterior or cleaning the interior will not affect your car price.
  • Stick to Professionals: The used car market can be an enticing option to sell your car, with dealers very often competing against each other to offer a great deal. However, selling in the unorganised market can lead to issues such as the car’s RC not being transferred to the new owner, which could land you in legal trouble.
